Varanasi is an ancient city located in south-eastern Uttar Pradesh state, northern India. It is situated on the left bank of the Ganges (Ganga) River and holds great religious importance for Hindus as one of the seven sacred cities. Varanasi attracts tourists from all over the world who come to experience its unique atmosphere and learn more about Hinduism. The city has been a centre of Vedic religion and philosophy for millennia, and it is also famous for its muslin and silk fabrics, perfumes, ivory works, and sculpture. 

Varanasi, a city of spiritualism where the people are living by following their great culture and traditions. People, who are living in this city consider themselves great disciples of Lord Shiva. They believe that they are blessed by God directly, that’s why they have the chance to live in Varanasi. People of Kashi really enjoy and live their cultural, traditional, spiritual and intellectual beliefs in God. The city serves its people and tourists with the immense love and rich natural resources. From the era, this city has been referred to as the city of temples, the holy city of God in India, the most religious city of India, the city of lights and learning, city of old culture and tradition and one of the oldest living cities on the earth. 

Varanasi is renowned for its riverfront, with miles of ghats (steps) for religious bathing and an array of shrines, temples, and palaces rising tier on tier from the water’s edge. The city’s inner streets are narrow, winding, and impassable for motor traffic; the newer outer suburbs are more spacious and are laid out more systematically. In terms of historical significance, Varanasi is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world and is also considered to be a holy city by Hindus, Buddhists, and Jains. 

Varanasi is an ancient city with a long and rich history, dating back to the time of the Buddha in the 6th century BCE. The city was the capital of the kingdom of Kashi during that time, and it was here that the Buddha gave his first sermon at Sarnath. Varanasi remained an important centre of religious, educational, and artistic activity for centuries after that, as attested by the famous Chinese Buddhist pilgrim Xuanzang, who visited it in about 635 CE. 

Among the city’s numerous temples, three of the most venerated are those of Vishvanatha (Vishwanath) dedicated to Shiva, Hanuman; and Durga. The Durga Temple is especially famous for the swarms of monkeys that inhabit the large trees near it. Visitors to the temple often feed the monkeys, which has led to some problems with the monkeys becoming aggressive and biting people. Other prominent religious buildings in the city include the Great Mosque of Aurangzeb and two modern temples – Tulasi Manas and Vishvanatha on the campus of Banaras Hindu University. 

For centuries, Varanasi has been a renowned center of Hindu learning and culture. There are countless schools and learned scholars who have kept traditional knowledge alive. Three universities are located in the city, including the world-famous Banaras Hindu University (1915). In addition, there are more than a dozen colleges and high schools. Varanasi is especially renowned for its arts and crafts, music and dance. The city is especially famous for its silk and brocade fabrics with gold and silver thread work.
